Part 1: Theoretical Analysis
Q1. How Edge AI reduces latency and enhances privacy (with real-world example)

Edge AI refers to deploying artificial intelligence models directly on local devices (edge devices) instead of sending data to remote cloud servers for processing.

How Edge AI Reduces Latency

Local computation:
Data is processed on-device, eliminating long round-trip communication to the cloud.

Faster inference times:
Because no network delays exist, inference occurs in milliseconds.

Greater reliability:
Works even with poor or no internet connectivity.

How Edge AI Enhances Privacy

Data stays on the device (e.g., camera, smartphone, sensor).
Sensitive information is not transmitted to external servers.

Reduced exposure to cyberattacks:
Since data is not stored in cloud databases, the risk of leaks is lower.

Local anonymization:
Edge devices can preprocess and remove personal data before sending summaries to the cloud.

Real-World Example: Autonomous Drones

Autonomous drones require:

Real-time obstacle detection

Navigation

Object recognition

Cloud computation would introduce delays and could cause crashes.
Edge AI enables drones to:

Process camera feeds instantly

Detect obstacles locally

Maintain safe flight paths

Protect captured video data by keeping it on the drone
This makes Edge AI essential for mission-critical robotics.

Q2. Quantum AI vs Classical AI for Optimization Problems
Classical AI

Classical AI algorithms (e.g., genetic algorithms, gradient descent, simulated annealing) run on traditional computers using binary logic.

They struggle when:

Data becomes extremely large

Optimization landscapes have many local minima

Combinatorial problems grow exponentially

Quantum AI uses quantum computing principles like:

Quantum computers can search vast solution spaces faster and more efficiently.
